The story

Past his due date

01

The diagnosis

David went undiagnosed for the first seven years of his life. His mother Eithna kept pushing, through specialists and second opinions, until the answer came back: cystic fibrosis. Childhood became hospital stays, missed school, and a routine of around thirty tablets a day.

At twelve he googled the condition and read a life expectancy of mid-twenties. He is twenty-seven now.

02

The mechanic

Golf started as pitch and putt with his father John and his brothers. In 2019 he beat his dad in the final of the Muskerry Junior Scratch Cup and sensed there might be more in it. For five years he worked as a mechanic at Audi Cork, playing his first full season of championships around the day job in 2023, then went full time in 2024.

Three years ago his handicap was not low enough for the Munster Strokeplay field. In 2025 he won the East of Ireland Championship at Baltray and earned his first Irish cap.

03

The Open

He reached Final Qualifying as first reserve, travelling to Scotland by ferry with John in the camper van they call the Wanderly Wagon. Two rounds later he was holding an Open pin flag. At Royal Birkdale he joins Rory McIlroy, Shane Lowry and Pádraig Harrington in the field at the oldest championship in golf.

The home team

John on the bag. Eithna, who fought seven years for the answer. Michael and Jack, a PGA professional, in the crowd behind. Golf in this house was never a solo game.

65 Roses

Playing for more than a scorecard

David has been a Cystic Fibrosis Ireland ambassador since 2024. The mission is simple: show every child growing up with CF that anything is possible.

This spring he took on the 65 Roses challenge his own way, covering 65 kilometres on the golf course and pledging money for every birdie along the road. During Open week, every eye on David is an eye on the CF community.
